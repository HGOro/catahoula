Xoloitzcuintli Facts

  • Xoloitzcuintli = xolotl + itzcuintli.

    Xolotl is the Aztec god of life and death. Itzcuintli is nahuatl for "dog."

  • These doggos are most recognizable for having little to no fur.

    Whereas many other hairless dogs and cats are a result of humans breeding them, the Xoloitzcuintli developed with little human intervention.

  • The breed's existence can be traced back 3000 years.

    As hinted in the previous fact, Xolos are a primitive breed. Depictions of these intelligent, independent dogs date as far back as pre-Colombian times.

  • With origins rooted in Mexico, their presence and influence has been well documented in the region's culture.

    • In Aztec mythology, Xolos were considered companions to the dead on the journey to the underworld.
    • Throughout Mexican history, Xolos have inspired many artists - painters, authors, photographers - and have become a national symbol.
    • Most recently, the Xolo has experienced a world-wide rise in popularity due to a lovable Xolo's role in Disney's Coco.
